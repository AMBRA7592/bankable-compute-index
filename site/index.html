Bankable Compute Index

Version 0.1 · May 2026 · View source on GitHub

Thesis: the scarce unit in AI infrastructure is not data or generic compute. It is bankable compute — powered, permitted, networked accelerator capacity with a credible delivery date and a customer contract strong enough to support external financing.

This is a methodology, not a ranking. v0.1 ships an inspectable framework with explicit confidence grades and visible missing data. Disagreement with the weighting or definitions is a feature, not a bug.

Four quality grades

  • Powered vs promised — Is the megawatt actually energised, or only announced?
  • Priority access vs generic — Does the buyer sit at the top of an allocation hierarchy, or merely on a waitlist?
  • Contract-backed vs speculative — Is the revenue claim creditworthy and term-defined, or merchant exposure?
  • Refreshable vs stranded — Is there a credible hardware-refresh path, or will the asset be obsolete before debt amortises?

Six scorecard lines

  1. Power deliverability — firm, timely, price-competitive power for high-density loads
  2. Accelerator access — packaged GPUs or ASICs available by date, not purchase intent
  3. HBM and advanced packaging — high-bandwidth memory and CoWoS-class packaging
  4. Sites and networks — permitted campuses with cooling, fiber, interconnection
  5. Creditworthy offtake — take-or-pay contracts and customer-credit depth
  6. Operating talent — semiconductor, power, grid, data-centre operating capability
